About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Utrecht University, where my research focuses on investigating the capabilities of Large Language Models, particularly in learning impossible languages. My work sits at the intersection of theoretical linguistics and artificial intelligence, exploring fundamental questions about language acquisition and computational learning.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I gained extensive industry experience as an NLP/ML researcher and Big Data Engineer, where I developed scalable systems for information retrieval, search engines, and data pipelines. My technical expertise spans transformer architectures (BERT, GPT), distributed systems, and cloud infrastructure.
